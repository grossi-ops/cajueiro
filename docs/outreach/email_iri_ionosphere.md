**The core idea.** We treat Schumann resonances in the Earth–ionosphere cavity and
acoustic eigenmodes in Hypogeum-type chambers as two realizations of the identical
Helmholtz eigenproblem

  ℒ(Ω, ∂Ω; BC) ψₙ = λₙ ψₙ

with all geometry and boundary conditions generated by a fixed operator chain
G = U ∘ F ∘ K ∘ C (Compress → Curvature → Fold → Unfold).

**The key result we need your help with.** Our notebook
`schumann_real_variability.ipynb` currently models ionospheric height variability
with a simple parametric proxy (±5 km day/night, ±2.5 km solar-cycle, drawn from
Williams, Nickolaenko, and Sátori). This reproduces the right order-of-magnitude
behavior, but it is not physics: we need real IRI-2016 profiles.

**Open roadmap Issue #1** in the repository is exactly this:

> Integrate IRI-2016 (via `iricore` or `PyIRI`) so that the effective height
> h′(f) at 7.83 Hz is computed dynamically from date/time/location, and overlaid
> on the Schumann variability figure with a secondary axis.

The result would be a notebook that any RF or atmospheric scientist can open,
change the date and solar flux, and immediately see how the dm³ curvature
parameter κ maps to a real IRI ionospheric profile — and how that same κ-range
explains the 10–25 cm wall sensitivity measured in a Neolithic stone chamber
(Wolfe et al. 2020, arXiv:2010.13697).
